What are GDG and DevFest?

DevFest is the flagship developer conference organized by GDG chapters worldwide. DevFest events are typically day-long or multi-day gatherings featuring keynote talks, technical sessions, workshops, and hands-on labs. Themes often include web, cloud, mobile, machine learning, and developer tooling.

Hosted by Google Developer Groups (GDG), these events foster local developer communities and encourage inclusion and learning.

Google Developer Groups (GDG)

GDG (Google Developer Groups) are community groups for developers who are interested in Google developer technologies. They are community-run chapters where developers meet to learn, share knowledge, and build real projects together. Chapters typically run regular meetups, codelabs, and mentoring sessions to help members improve their technical skills, network, and collaborate on open-source initiatives.

Common GDG activities include workshops, speaker sessions, hackathons, and networking. GDG fosters local developer communities and encourages inclusion and learning.

DevFest

For more about GDG and DevFest, see the official GDG page and local chapter announcements. (See: https://developers.google.com/community)

How to participate

  • Join your local GDG chapter and attend meetups.
  • Volunteer to speak, organize events or run workshops.
  • Contribute to community projects and open-source demos.

Community values

GDG chapters prioritize inclusivity, learning, and hands-on practice. They welcome developers of all skill levels and backgrounds.

Find events & get involved

Look up local chapters and upcoming DevFest events on the official community pages. Many chapters post event schedules, volunteer opportunities, and codelabs for newcomers. If no chapter exists in your area you can apply to start one.
